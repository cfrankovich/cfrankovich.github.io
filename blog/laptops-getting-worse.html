laptops are getting worse.

New technology is interesting. Whether you like it or not is a completely different story but I think we can all agree that looking at new things is always cool. Today I was interested in what's new so I went to one of my favorite tech sites, Ars Technica, and I one of the featured articles was the new Dell XPS 15 9510. Curious, I visit the page and was soon in shock from the subtitle that stated "Limited ports and a midrange GPU don't detract much from an excellent laptop." This blog is just going to me ranting on how laptops are getting worse and worse.

First off, all modern laptops' goal is to be thin and weigh almost nothing. There are a couple of problems when it comes to a thinner laptop. The largest problem in my opinion is actually the keyboard. If a user is going to be using the laptop for an extended use of time, the keyboard must be comfy to type on. For example, if you own a thinner laptop type a sentence or two out and then do the same think but on your bare desk. It almost feels the same minus the actual feeling of the keyboard. Doing this for a long period of time can really put a strain on your fingers and eventually get tiring. I own four laptops and each one has a different keyboard feel and coincidentally, the thickest laptop has the best typing experience since there is more room to improve the keyboard. Again, it doesn't feel like I'm typing on my desk, it feels like an actual keyboard. If you ever see my in person with my laptop ask me to type on my laptop and you will be enlightened (not really but trust me its great). I should really divide these reasons in paragraphs but whatever. The next one is that cooling is HORRIBLE. If you own a macbook or another really thin laptop you know exactly what I'm talking about. The cooling solutions on a sleek and thin laptop are generally non existent. There is no room for a clean and efficient cooling solution, especially when doing heavy tasks like video rendering and gaming. This could be argued since there are great thermal engineers that have designed efficient cooling systems for thin laptops. I would also like to point out why do people want their laptops to weigh like a feather? Is it to heavy to carry around? What if you were carrying the laptop in your backpack and a thief comes to steal it? It would be hard to tell that your laptop has been stolen since the weight difference is almost nothing compared to other things one might keep in their backpack. Additionally since the laptop is lightweight, the more accident prone it is as well. Dropping something heavy is generally harder to do than dropping something light. This all depends on personal preference though so there is no point in arguing against someone's choice.

More and more ports are leaving! This could be taken into account with laptops becoming thinner but I believe that less ports deserve their own paragraph. I don't believe that this "feature" applies to normies as much as I think it does. Everyone now uses the cloud and throws their data in the hands of large corporations in hopes of it not getting lost, stolen, hacked, or leaked. Cloud computing deserves its own blog but no one wants to use external hard drives or even a USB now! I'm not going to go over the main advantages of using USB's but the main one in my opinion is making a bootable usb to install linux. Besides that, even video ports like HDMI, DP, and VGA are now non existent on these computers. What if you need to plug in your laptop to present to a class or business executives? You have to pull out your 23 adapters you bought on amazon and get the right one. It's honestly getting to a point where the only port a laptop will have by 2040 is a charging port (all jokes). Why do companies want to remove important daily-use ports now? I won't ever get it and I refuse to buy a laptop that doesn't even have a USB port. That is ridiculous.

This paragraph kind of dives into my reasoning for the x220 but newer laptops aren't easy to repair and upgrade. On my personal laptop, I can access the RAM, hard drive, and battery without having to open up the whole laptop. If one part breaks or I want to replace it, it is easy to do so. With a maximum of two screws to undo. You can see what I mean by viewing the image bellow.

I've heard an argument saying that an attacker can just take the battery out of my computer if they wanted to and this is true but why on earth would I leave my computer unattended? Plus there is a safety lock for the battery so it would take the attacker a second or two to snatch the battery if they really wanted to. And why take the battery out if you can just take the whole laptop? Makes no sense to me but yea sure... someone can take the battery if I was not looking and they were dumb enough to target the battery instead of the computer itself.

To wrap things up, technology advancing is a great thing but we can't trade good features for the "next best thing." I would love to see a company bring back old style laptops back to the market in the future with modern components.
